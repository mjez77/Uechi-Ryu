The Way of Uechi-ryu

Founder Kanbun Uechi (1877–1948)

A Legacy from Okinawa

Originally called Pangai-noon (half-hard, half-soft), the style was brought from China to Okinawa by Kanbun Uechi at the turn of the 20th century. Later refined by his son, Kanei Uechi, it became one of the three main karate styles of Okinawa.

Uechi-ryu is unique for its use of open-hand techniques, low kicks, and a strong upright stance. It is heavily influenced by three animal archetypes: The Tiger (strength), The Crane (balance), and The Dragon (explosive power).

Sanchin

The foundational kata of Uechi-ryu. It focuses on deep abdominal breathing, a rooted stance, and body structure. It is the key to developing internal power, concentration, and dynamic tension.

Conditioning

Traditional body conditioning (Kote Kitae). Through progressive impact training, students develop "iron-like" forearms and legs, essential for both effective defense and explosive offense.

Self-Defense

Uechi-ryu focuses on practical, close-quarters combat. We utilize open-hand techniques, low kicks, and finger/palm strikes to vital points with maximum efficiency and realistic scenarios.
